A complete end-to-end **SaaS Business Analytics Project** using SQL — analyzing customer behavior, churn, revenue trends, upgrades/downgrades, segmentation, and product engagement.  

This project mirrors real-world SaaS KPIs used by Data Analysts, Product Analysts, and Business Intelligence teams.

---

## 🚀 **Project Overview**
This project explores a full SaaS dataset and answers 10 industry-level business questions through SQL.  
It demonstrates strong analytical thinking, metric-building ability, and SQL proficiency.

---

## 📂 **About the Dataset**
The dataset represents typical SaaS business operations and includes:

- **subscriptions** → MRR, ARR, plan tier, revenue  
- **accounts** → customer info  
- **support_tickets** → response time, satisfaction score  
- **feature_usage** → feature adoption, engagement  
- **churn_events** → churn reasons, refund amounts  

A perfect dataset to practice **real SaaS KPIs**.

## 🔟 **Breakdown of the 10 Queries (Explanation Only)**

### **1️⃣ Monthly Recurring Revenue Trend**  
Shows how MRR changes month-by-month to track growth and seasonal shifts.

### **2️⃣ Customer Churn Analysis**  
Identifies why customers cancel and which reason contributes most to churn.

### **3️⃣ Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**  
Ranks customers by total revenue generated (MRR + ARR).

### **4️⃣ Churn Risk Prediction**  
Flags customers as High/Medium/Low risk using support activity + downgrade signals.

### **5️⃣ Feature Adoption & Engagement**  
Measures which features are used most and their adoption percentages.

### **6️⃣ Support Quality & Impact**  
Evaluates how support response time and satisfaction affect retention.

### **7️⃣ Subscription Plan Performance**  
Compares Basic, Pro, Enterprise plans by revenue and customer count.

### **8️⃣ Customer Segmentation by Behavior**  
Groups customers into High Value, Mid Tier, and Low Risk segments.

### **9️⃣ Upgrade & Downgrade Trends**  
Analyzes account movement between plans and revenue expansion vs contraction.

### **🔟 Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**  
Shows if revenue from existing customers grew or declined month-over-month.

## 📘 **Theory & Best Practices**
- Always pair MRR with customer count for accuracy  
- Use window functions for retention & trend metrics  
- Segmentation must combine both spending + behavior  
- Churn should always include percentage analysis  
- Support metrics influence customer lifetime value
